Canada Visa FAQs

Everything you need to know about applying for a visa to Canada.

To apply for a Canada Student Visa (Study Permit), you generally need an acceptance letter from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI), a valid passport, and proof of sufficient financial support to cover your tuition and living expenses. You may also need to provide a letter of explanation and medical exams.
Yes, most international students can work in Canada. You are generally allowed to work up to 20 hours per week during regular school terms, and full-time during scheduled academic breaks (like winter and summer holidays).
Processing times can vary significantly depending on your location, the type of visa, and the time of year. Generally, study permit applications can take anywhere from 4 to 12 weeks. We recommend starting the process well in advance of your planned travel date.
Yes! If you are studying full-time at an eligible DLI, your spouse or common-law partner may be eligible to apply for an open work permit, allowing them to work for any employer in Canada while you study.
